Teleprompter vs Cue Cards: Which is Better?

7 min read

Both teleprompters and cue cards help speakers deliver prepared content. But they serve different purposes and suit different situations. Whether you're filming a corporate video in Columbus, hosting a live event in Cleveland, or producing content in Cincinnati, here's how to choose the right tool for your Ohio production.

Teleprompter Advantages

  • Direct eye contact with camera or audience
  • Can handle long, complex scripts
  • Easy real-time script changes
  • Consistent scroll speed
  • Professional, polished appearance

Cue Card Advantages

  • Lower cost for simple productions
  • No technology to fail
  • Can work without power
  • Visible gestures from card holder can cue timing

When to Use Teleprompters

For most professional productions across Ohio—from corporate headquarters in Cleveland to convention centers in Columbus to broadcast studios in Cincinnati—teleprompters are the industry standard.

  • On-camera work requiring eye contact
  • Long scripts (more than a few sentences)
  • Live events with multiple speakers
  • Professional productions with budgets
  • Any situation where looking polished matters

When Cue Cards Work

  • Brief bullet points only
  • Talk show style with visible personality
  • Very low budget productions
  • Outdoor locations without power

Ohio Production Considerations

Ohio's diverse production landscape—from Rock and Roll Hall of Fame events in Cleveland to Ohio State corporate partnerships in Columbus to P&G headquarters productions in Cincinnati—demands flexibility. Indoor conference centers favor teleprompters, while outdoor stadium events at FirstEnergy Stadium or Ohio Stadium may require backup plans.

The Verdict

For professional productions, teleprompters are almost always the better choice. The eye contact advantage alone—being able to look directly at your audience while delivering scripted content—is transformative.
